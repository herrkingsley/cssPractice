The last years I have been watching a lot of trees. The trunks and the bark remind me of skin and I can’t help myself from fantasizing about the different personalities of the trees. The tall majestic ones, the young, fresh competing for space and sunlight, and the old hooky – those who have struggled through rain and storms since the time before my grandfather was even born. What have they seen? Who did they meet? Sometimes I climb up and I stroke my hands over the rugged surface covering the life giving system of vessels and fibres. I know my feelings are probably not returned but I often feel like the tree is hugging me back – at least I hope it doesn’t mind me sitting
